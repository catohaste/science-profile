Research

  • PhD rotation projects during the academic year 2017/2018.
    During the first year of my PhD I will complete three 12 week-long research projects. The aim is to get a taste for what different labs feel like to better help the choice of my final PhD project and supervisor.
    I am currently analysing protein-protein interaction networks in Christine Orengo's group, with the aim of identifying driver proteins in bladder cancer.
  • Multiple projects undertaken during the 2016/17 academic year.
    See the MRes page for more details.
  • Research placement with Oxford e-Research Centre, funded by Nvidia, during the summer of 2013.
    I worked with a partner to develop an innovative program implementing the Thomas algorithm rapidly solving large systems of linear equations. The program was written using CUDA(/C++) and provided my introduction to scientific programming.
  • Undergraduate project in mathematical modelling and numerical computation, over the academic year 2012/13.
    I conducted novel research modelling pressure changes in the cerebrospinal fluid in patients with hydrocephalus. I submitted a 7500 word report and presented to academic staff, achieving a 1st class mark.
